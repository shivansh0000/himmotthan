Overview

Himmotthan Society is dedicated to empowering mountain communities in Uttarakhand and neighboring regions through sustainable development initiatives. By focusing on natural resource management, livelihood enhancement, education, water and sanitation, and disaster rehabilitation, Himmotthan works collaboratively with partners, government agencies, and local institutions to create resilient, self-sustained communities that thrive in harmony with their environment.

Vision

Together with the partner organizations, Himmotthan is dedicated to the vision of building prosperous and self sustained mountain communities which live in harmony and sympathy with their natural surroundings.

Livestock Initiative

Livestock has traditionally been an integral component of the hill farming system in Uttarakhand, contributing significantly to the rural economy through household income, family nutrition, biomass production, and draught power for agricultural activities. For many rural households, especially marginal farmers, livestock serves as the primary source of income. However, the productivity of milch animals in the central and western Himalayan regions remains low due to limited availability of high-quality green fodder.

×

Agriculture Initiative

Given the distinct ecosystem of the hill regions, agricultural development in the hill districts of Uttarakhand requires a tailored approach. Farmers here primarily engage in subsistence agriculture, hindered by small and scattered landholdings, difficult terrain, lack of irrigation, limited access to improved inputs and technology, and poor credit and marketing facilities.

×

Water and Sanitation Initiative

'Improving Community Health through Drinking Water Supply and Sanitation' is a flagship initiative under the Himmotthan Mountain Program (HMP), aimed at enhancing community health by implementing village-level drinking water and environmental sanitation projects with a strong emphasis on hygiene.

Education Institution

Himmotthan has developed a deep understanding of the educational challenges and community needs across the Central and Western Himalayan regions. At the core of its strategy is the goal of ensuring quality education for all children, particularly those from marginalized backgrounds.

Institution and Enterprise

The livelihoods of rural communities in Uttarakhand's Central Himalayan region are primarily subsistence-based, relying on agriculture, livestock, and forest resources. However, challenges such as small and fragmented landholdings, traditional farming practices, degradation of natural resources have led to persistent rural poverty.

Post Disaster Rehabilitation

The Himalayas have always been vulnerable to the vagaries of many natural disasters like earthquakes due to plate tectonic movements, cloudbursts, flash floods, avalanches and landslides. All these natural disasters result in bringing the government and various organizations to put in their efforts in coordination for immediate relief.
